## About Writing Contest 2026

Writing Contest 2026 is a practical Cloud Native case-study event in the Writing Hub.
The goal is simple: publish real engineering stories that others can reuse in production.

- Event window: 14/03/2026 - 30/04/2026 (online)
- Final submission deadline: 30/04/2026
- Recommended article length: 1,000-1,500 words
- Language: English / Vietnamese

## Who Should Join

- Kubernetes practitioners and platform engineers
- SRE and DevOps engineers
- Developers working with cloud native tooling
- Anyone with practical stories and lessons learned

## Suggested Topics

- Production case studies and incident learnings
- GitOps workflows and delivery practices
- Observability, reliability, and performance tuning
- Security hardening and cost optimization

## How The Event Unfolds

1. Prepare your case-study idea and draft your article in Markdown.
2. Submit during the event window with a Pull Request.
3. Receive review feedback in around 3-5 working days.
4. Update your draft in the same PR until it meets quality requirements.
5. Approved posts are published and highlighted in community channels.
6. Final evaluation happens after the deadline and top entries are announced.

## Suggested Article Structure

1. Context and problem
2. Solution and architecture decisions
3. Implementation details (config, snippets, lessons)
4. Results and measurable impact
5. Conclusion and references

## Submission Note

For this event, place your post file in:

`_articles/writing-hub/writing-contest-2026/`

Then open a Pull Request with title format:

`Writing Hub: <your article title>`

You can submit more than one article if each post has unique practical value.

**Note**: All posts submitted to the contest must include the tag `writing-contest-2026-term1`.
